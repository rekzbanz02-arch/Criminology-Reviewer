📖 Study Notes
  • Criminology = scientific study of crime, criminals, victims + prevention and solution
  • RA 11131 includes victims (Important trick item)
  • ❌ NOT limited only to penalties
  • Tip: If "victims excluded" appears → mark it WRONG
  • Classical: Free will, clear laws, fixed punishment
  • Neoclassical: Age + mental condition considered
  • Positivist: No free will — behavior shaped by biology & environment
  • Key Pattern:
    • Judge considers age → Neoclassical
    • Crime due to external factors → Positivist
    • Clear fixed law, equal punishment → Classical
  • Superego = moral control (RIGHT vs WRONG)
  • Dopamine = pleasure → drives addiction and crime repetition
  • Defense Mechanisms:
    • Displacement → redirects anger to others
    • Sublimation → turns aggression into constructive activity (e.g., sports)
    • Fantasy → mental escape from reality
  • Personality Disorders:
    • Paranoid → extreme distrust of others
    • Narcissistic → sense of superiority, lack of empathy
    • Histrionic → attention-seeking behavior
    • Dependent → inability to make independent decisions
  • Broken Windows Theory: Visible disorder in the environment invites crime
  • Routine Activity Theory:
    • Crime occurs when: motivated offender + suitable target + absence of capable guardian
  • Crime Generator: High-crowd areas produce more criminal opportunities
  • Cultural Transmission: Criminal behavior is learned from the environment
  • Piaget:
    • Sensorimotor stage → infants explore using senses
    • Assimilation → applying existing knowledge to new situations
  • Erikson:
    • Industry vs. Inferiority → failure in school shapes self-image
    • Generativity → productive contribution as an adult
  • Victim Precipitation: Victim's behavior contributes to or provokes the crime
  • Victim Profiling: Analysis of lifestyle and risk factors
  • WPP (Witness Protection Program): Protects witnesses to encourage testimony
  • Delicadeza: Avoid conflict of interest; sense of propriety
  • Integrity: Honesty at all times
  • Devotion to Duty: No sleeping on duty, no neglect
  • Grass Eaters: Officers who accept small, passive bribes
  • Meat Eaters: Officers who actively seek large corrupt gains
  • Tip: If a gift is accepted → Delicadeza is violated
  • Main Goal: Rehabilitation, NOT punishment
  • Diversion: Settlement without going to court
  • Suspended Sentence: Found guilty but not sent to prison
  • Separation: Minors must be kept separate from adult offenders
  • Lupong Tagapamayapa: Main body overseeing barangay justice
  • Pangkat: Panel that handles specific disputes
  • Arbitration: Binding decision rendered by the arbitrator
  • Mediation: Neutral party guides discussion; decision not binding
  • Conciliation: Helps both parties reach a voluntary agreement
  • Tip: Cases involving the government → barangay conciliation does NOT apply
  • Descriptive Statistics: Summarize and describe data only
  • Inferential Statistics: Draw conclusions and make predictions
  • Mean: The computed average of values
  • Empiricism: Knowledge based on actual evidence and observation
  • Correlational Study: Examines relationship between variables
  • Quasi-Experiment: No random assignment of participants
  • Look for keywords in each question (age, environment, crowd, etc.)
  • Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
  • For scenario-based items → match the theory
  • For ethics items → almost always Integrity or Delicadeza
  • For juvenile items → always Rehabilitation
Golden Rule: Understand the scenario — don't just memorize blindly. Focus on patterns that repeat in board exams and use trigger words for fast recall under pressure.
